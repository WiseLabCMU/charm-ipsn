Low-Power Wide Area Networks (LP-WANs) are an emerging wireless platform designed to enable long-range communication at low data rates to tens of thousands of battery-operated sensing devices. Unlike in cellular networks where towers are strategically placed, the uncoordinated nature of LP-WANs compounded with their long transmission range can yield poor overall network capacity.
